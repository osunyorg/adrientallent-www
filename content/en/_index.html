On December 15, 2025, I defended my PhD thesis at Sorbonne University (Science, Norms, and Democracy Laboratory SND - UMR8011) on the ethical and political consequences of the massive use of data by states and companies and its processing by artificial intelligence systems for governance purposes. I am particularly interested in the possibilities for democratic renewal (participation, decentralization, etc.) in the digital context. In short, I am questioning the notion of the social contract in the digital age. During my thesis, I benefited from a three-year CIFRE doctoral contract with SNCF Réseau, which allowed me to combine my theoretical and philosophical interest in digital technology and AI with a practical perspective. 


Doctor in Political Philosophy and
ethics, ethic teacher in digital ethics and AI, and writer,
is currently preparing a philosophy thesis at Sorbonne University on
the ethical and political consequences of the massive use of
Member States and enterprises and their processing by
artificial intelligence systems for governance purposes. In short,
I question the notion of a social contract in the digital age.


During my PhD, I had the opportunity to present my work on numerous occasions in French and English (at conferences, congresses, in publications, etc.) to a wide variety of audiences (researchers, teachers, high school students, university students, professionals, the general public, etc.). I co-edited the April 2025 issue of the journal Esprit dedicated to artificial intelligence (I had previously organized and led a working group on AI for this journal) and a book on the French philosopher Jacques Ellul (Avec Jacques Ellul, Labor et Fides, 2025). I have gained teaching experience through lectures (on the ethics of digital technology/AI and the philosophy of AI, in French and English) and seminars at university level. I have had the opportunity to supervise students in their master's theses (at Neoma Business School).  Finally, in 2024, I had the opportunity to be a visiting researcher at Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität in Munich, where I worked with the research team on the ethics of artificial intelligence under the supervision of Professor Sven Nyholm.
